Worked Examples

Part A — Same Denominator

💡 When fractions have the same denominator, just add or subtract the numerators. The denominator stays the same.

Example 1 — Addition: \(\dfrac{3}{8} + \dfrac{2}{8}\)

Step 1 Same denominator (8) — add the numerators: \(3 + 2 = \mathbf{5}\).

Step 2 Keep the denominator: \(\dfrac{5}{8}\).

Step 3 Check if it simplifies — 5 and 8 share no common factors, so it's already in simplest form.

Answer: \(\dfrac{5}{8}\)

Example 2 — Subtraction: \(\dfrac{7}{10} - \dfrac{3}{10}\)

Step 1 Same denominator (10) — subtract numerators: \(7 - 3 = \mathbf{4}\).

Step 2 Result: \(\dfrac{4}{10}\).

Step 3 Simplify — both 4 and 10 divide by 2: \(\dfrac{4}{10} = \mathbf{\dfrac{2}{5}}\).

Answer: \(\dfrac{2}{5}\)

Part B — Different Denominators

💡 When the denominators are different, find the Lowest Common Denominator (LCD), convert both fractions, then add or subtract.

Example 3 — Addition: \(\dfrac{1}{3} + \dfrac{1}{4}\)

Step 1 Find the LCD of 3 and 4. Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12… Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12… LCD = 12.

Step 2 Convert each fraction: \(\dfrac{1}{3} = \dfrac{4}{12}\)  |  \(\dfrac{1}{4} = \dfrac{3}{12}\).

Step 3 Add: \(\dfrac{4}{12} + \dfrac{3}{12} = \dfrac{7}{12}\).

Step 4 7 and 12 share no factors — already simplified.

Answer: \(\dfrac{7}{12}\)

Example 4 — Subtraction: \(\dfrac{3}{4} - \dfrac{1}{3}\)

Step 1 LCD of 4 and 3 = 12.

Step 2 Convert: \(\dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{9}{12}\)  |  \(\dfrac{1}{3} = \dfrac{4}{12}\).

Step 3 Subtract: \(\dfrac{9}{12} - \dfrac{4}{12} = \dfrac{5}{12}\).

Answer: \(\dfrac{5}{12}\)
