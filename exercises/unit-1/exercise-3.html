Worked Examples

Part A — Same Denominator

💡 When fractions have the same denominator, just add or subtract the numerators. The denominator stays the same.

Example 1 — Addition: 3/8 + 2/8

Step 1 The denominators are the same (8), so add the numerators: 3 + 2 = 5.

Step 2 Keep the denominator: 5/8.

Step 3 Check if it simplifies — 5 and 8 share no common factors, so it's already in simplest form.

Answer: 5/8

Example 2 — Subtraction: 7/10 − 3/10

Step 1 Same denominator (10), subtract numerators: 7 − 3 = 4.

Step 2 Result: 4/10.

Step 3 Simplify — both 4 and 10 divide by 2: 4/10 = 2/5.

Answer: 2/5

Part B — Different Denominators

💡 When the denominators are different, find the Lowest Common Denominator (LCD), convert both fractions, then add or subtract.

Example 3 — Addition: 1/3 + 1/4

Step 1 Find the LCD of 3 and 4. Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12… Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12… LCD = 12.

Step 2 Convert each fraction: 1/3 = 4/12  |  1/4 = 3/12.

Step 3 Add: 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12.

Step 4 Check — 7 and 12 share no factors, already simplified.

Answer: 7/12

Example 4 — Subtraction: 3/4 − 1/3

Step 1 LCD of 4 and 3 = 12.

Step 2 Convert: 3/4 = 9/12  |  1/3 = 4/12.

Step 3 Subtract: 9/12 − 4/12 = 5/12.

Answer: 5/12
