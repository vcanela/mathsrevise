Worked Examples

Part A: Multiplying Fractions

📌 The Rule
Multiply the numerators together, then multiply the denominators together: \(\dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a \times c}{b \times d}\) then simplify if possible.

Example 1: \(\dfrac{2}{3} \times \dfrac{3}{5}\)

Step 1 Multiply numerators: \(2 \times 3 = \mathbf{6}\).

Step 2 Multiply denominators: \(3 \times 5 = \mathbf{15}\).

Step 3 Result: \(\dfrac{6}{15}\).

Step 4 Simplify — divide both by 3: \(\dfrac{6}{15} = \dfrac{2}{5}\).

Answer: \(\dfrac{2}{5}\)

Part B: Dividing Fractions

📌 The Rule “Keep, Change, Flip”
Keep the first fraction.  Change ÷ to ×.  Flip the second fraction (its reciprocal).

\(\dfrac{a}{b} \div \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{d}{c}\) then multiply and simplify as normal.

Example 2: \(\dfrac{3}{4} \div \dfrac{2}{3}\)

Keep Keep the first fraction: \(\dfrac{3}{4}\).

Change Change \(\div\) to \(\times\).

Flip Flip the second fraction: \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) becomes \(\dfrac{3}{2}\).

Multiply \(\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{3}{2} = \dfrac{9}{8}\).

Answer: \(\dfrac{9}{8}\)
