Worked Examples

Part A — Multiplying Fractions

📌 The Rule
Multiply the numerators together, then multiply the denominators together.
a/b × c/d = (a×c) / (b×d)
Then simplify if possible.

Example 1 — 2/3 × 3/5

Step 1 Multiply the numerators: 2 × 3 = 6.

Step 2 Multiply the denominators: 3 × 5 = 15.

Step 3 Result: 6/15.

Step 4 Simplify — both 6 and 15 divide by 3: 6/15 = 2/5.

Answer: 2/5

💡 Tip — cancel before you multiply! If any numerator shares a factor with any denominator, cancel first to keep numbers smaller. For 2/3 × 3/5: the 3 in the numerator and the 3 in the denominator cancel, leaving 2/1 × 1/5 = 2/5. Same answer, easier arithmetic!

Part B — Dividing Fractions

📌 The Rule — "Keep, Change, Flip"
Keep the first fraction as it is.
Change the ÷ to ×.
Flip the second fraction (swap numerator and denominator — this is called the reciprocal).

a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c
Then multiply and simplify as normal.

Example 2 — 3/4 ÷ 2/3

Keep Keep the first fraction: 3/4.

Change Change ÷ to ×.

Flip Flip the second fraction: 2/3 becomes 3/2.

Multiply Now multiply: 3/4 × 3/2 = (3×3)/(4×2) = 9/8.

Simplify 9 and 8 share no common factors — already in simplest form.

Answer: 9/8  (or 1 and 1/8 as a mixed number)
