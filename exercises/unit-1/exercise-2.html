Worked Examples

💡 Key idea: Ignore the decimal points, multiply the whole numbers, then put the decimal point back in. Count the total number of decimal places in the question — your answer needs that many too.

Example 1 — 2.4 × 1.5

Step 1 Remove decimals: treat as 24 × 15.

Step 2 Multiply: 24 × 15 = 360.

Step 3 Count decimal places: 2.4 has 1 d.p., 1.5 has 1 d.p. → total 2 d.p.

Step 4 Place the decimal: 360 → 3.60

Answer: 3.60

Example 2 — 0.3 × 0.7

Step 1 Remove decimals: treat as 3 × 7.

Step 2 Multiply: 3 × 7 = 21.

Step 3 Count decimal places: 0.3 has 1 d.p., 0.7 has 1 d.p. → total 2 d.p.

Step 4 Place the decimal: 21 → 0.21

Answer: 0.21
