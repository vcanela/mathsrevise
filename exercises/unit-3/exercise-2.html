Worked Examples

Example 1: Angles in a triangle sum to 180°

The three interior angles of any triangle always add up to 180°.

Example 2: Finding the missing angle in a triangle

A triangle has angles 65° and 80°. Find the third angle.

Step 1 Angles in a triangle sum to 180°.

Step 2 Third angle = 180° \(-\) 65° \(-\) 80° = 35°

Example 3: Vertically opposite angles

When two straight lines cross, the angles opposite each other are equal.

Vertically opposite angles (formed when two straight lines cross) are always equal.
Hint: For the missing angle in a triangle, subtract the two known angles from 180°.
