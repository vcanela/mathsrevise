Example 1: What is a net?

A net is a flat 2D shape that can be folded up to make a 3D shape. Every face of the 3D shape appears as a flat panel in the net.

For example, a cube has 6 square faces. Its net is a cross of 6 squares:

Example 2: Scale factors

A scale factor tells you how much bigger (or smaller) a shape has become. Multiply every length by the scale factor to find the new dimensions.

Step 1 Identify the scale factor: scale factor = new length ÷ original length.

Step 2 Multiply all lengths by the scale factor to get the enlarged shape.

Scale factor = new length ÷ original length
To enlarge: multiply all lengths by the scale factor.
To reduce: scale factor is less than 1.
