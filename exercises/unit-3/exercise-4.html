Example 1: All parts of a circle

PartDescriptionKey fact
CentreMiddle point of the circleLabel it O
RadiusLine from centre to edgeradius = diameter ÷ 2
DiameterLine through centre, edge to edgediameter = 2 × radius
ChordLine joining two points on the edge (not through centre)Always shorter than diameter
ArcPart of the circumferenceA semicircle is half the circumference
SectorPie-slice region bounded by two radii and an arcLike a pizza slice
CircumferenceThe perimeter (total boundary) of the circle\(C = \pi \times \text{diameter}\)
TangentLine that touches the circle at exactly one pointAlways perpendicular to the radius at that point
