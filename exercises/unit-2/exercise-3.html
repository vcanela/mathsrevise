Worked Examples

Rule: Factorising is the reverse of expanding. We look for the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of all terms and take it outside a bracket.
\(a(b + c)\)
Expanding
\(ab + ac\)
\(a(b + c)\)
Factorising
\(ab + ac\)

Example 1: \(6a + 9\)

Step 1 HCF of 6 and 9 = 3

Step 2 Rewrite: \(6a + 9 = 3 \times 2a + 3 \times 3\)

Step 3 Take out HCF: \(= 3(2a + 3)\)

Answer: \(3(2a + 3)\)

Example 2: \(12p - 8\)

Step 1 HCF of 12 and 8 = 4

Step 2 \(12p - 8 = 4(3p - 2)\)

Answer: \(4(3p - 2)\)
