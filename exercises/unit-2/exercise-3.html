Worked Examples

Rule: Factorising is the reverse of expanding. We look for the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of all terms and take it outside a bracket.
\(a(b + c)\)
Expanding
\(ab + ac\)
\(a(b + c)\)
Factorising
\(ab + ac\)

Example 1 — \(6a + 9\)

Step 1 List factors of 6: 1, 2, 3, 6.   List factors of 9: 1, 3, 9.   HCF = 3

Step 2 Rewrite each term as a multiple of 3:   \(6a + 9 = 3 \times 2a + 3 \times 3\)

Step 3 Take the HCF outside:   \(= 3(2a + 3)\)

Check Expand to verify: \(3(2a + 3) = 6a + 9\) ✓

Answer: \(3(2a + 3)\)

Example 2 — \(12p - 8\)

Step 1 HCF of 12 and 8 = 4

Step 2 \(12p - 8 = 4 \times 3p - 4 \times 2 = 4(3p - 2)\)

Answer: \(4(3p - 2)\)

Example 3 — Two variables: \(15m + 10n\)

Step 1 HCF of 15 and 10 = 5

Step 2 \(15m + 10n = 5 \times 3m + 5 \times 2n = 5(3m + 2n)\)

Answer: \(5(3m + 2n)\)
