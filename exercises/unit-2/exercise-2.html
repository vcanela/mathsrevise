Worked Examples

Rule: Each term inside the bracket gets multiplied by the number outside. Be careful with signs!

Example 1: Single bracket: \(3(2a + 5)\)

Step 1 The multiplier outside acts on every term inside.

3(2a + 5)
↓
↓
3 × 2a = 6a    3 × 5 = 15

Step 2 Write the products: \(6a + 15\)

Answer: \(6a + 15\)

Example 2: With a negative: \(4(3p - 2)\)

Step 1 Multiply each term inside the bracket by 4.

\(4 \times 3p = 12p\)    \(4 \times (-2) = -8\)

Answer: \(12p - 8\)

Example 3: Expand and collect: \(2(x + 3) + 3(x - 1)\)

Step 1 Expand each bracket separately. From first bracket:  \(2x + 6\)  |  From second bracket:  \(3x - 3\)

Step 2 Collect like terms: \((2x + 3x) + (6 - 3) = 5x + 3\)

Answer: \(5x + 3\)
